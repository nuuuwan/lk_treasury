PRESS RELEASE

Sri Lanka receives USD 25 million credit from IBRD of the WB forPPP projects

The Government has recognized that a strong Public- PrivateProgramme (PPP) in Sri Lanka would accelerate FDI and economic

growth and will supplement the Government’s limited fiscalcapacity to address the growing infrastructure needs in the countryand better manage its fiscal obligations. In this regard, the NationalAgency for Public Private Partnership (NAPPP) was established inthe Ministry of Finance.

The Government has approached to seek fiscal and technicalsupport from multi-lateral agencies to strengthen NAPPP withinternational experiences, for which the International Bank forReconstruction and Development (IBRD) of the World Bank Grouphas shown their interest to mobilize its funds through a credit ofUnited States Dollars 25 million to implement the above project.

The project development objective is to support the preparation ofPublic–Private Partnerships that will enable the Government of SriLanka to facilitate private sector financing for the development ofits priority infrastructure and services such as Transportation, Waterand Sanitization, Ports and Shipping, Tourism, InformationCommunication Technology and Digital Infrastructure, AffordableHousing, Special Economic Zones, and Power and Energy and allinfrastructure & services projects relevant to PPP. The overallresponsibility of the implementation of this project lies with theMinistry of Finance and this project will be implemented by theMinistry of Finance through its PPP arm National Agency for PublicPrivate Partnership (NAPPP) during 2019 -2023.

The signing of Loan Agreement took place today, at 1345 hours inWashington, D.C. on April 11, 2019, during the Spring Meetings ofWorld Bank and International Monetary Fund, scheduled from April8 to 14, 2019 in Washington DC, United States of America.Agreement was signed by Dr. R H S. Samaratunga, Secretary,Ministry of Finance on behalf of the Government of Sri Lanka andMr. Hartwig Schafer, Vice President for the South Asia Region, onbehalf of the World Bank Group.
